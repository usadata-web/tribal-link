How to read these rankings

Combined ranking is the most useful "size" measure for a tribal corporation — but apples-to-apples comparisons across tribes are imperfect. Some tribes disclose tribal-government revenue (gaming, hospitality, retail, real estate), others disclose holding-company revenue, and a few only disclose an "economic impact" figure that includes indirect effects. Where we use an economic-impact number, it is flagged. Federal ceiling figures are deterministic from USAspending.

Category labels

Each tribe is labeled by federal-bookings pattern across FY22-FY25:

  • mainstay — Consistent year-over-year federal bookings; established federal practice
  • up-and-comer — Recent (FY24-25) acceleration above prior trajectory; investing in federal growth
  • variable — Year-over-year swings without a clear directional trend
  • cliff-FY26 — Steep FY26 YTD drop vs FY25 run-rate; signals potential contract loss or pause
  • declining — Multi-year federal bookings decline

Methodology note

Federal data is deterministic: extracted from USAspending.gov bulk download, consolidated under each tribe's parent firm key (e.g., "NANA REGIONAL CORPORATION, INC." rolls up Akima and all subs). Field used: base_and_all_options_value. This is the bookings/ceiling figure, not revenue recognition.

Commercial revenue sources vary by tribe:

  • Alaska Native Corporations — annual 2025 Alaska Top 49ers list (AKBizMag), generally HIGH confidence.
  • Cherokee Nation — Cherokee Nation Businesses (CNB) FY24 State of the Company report.
  • Chickasaw Nation — 2025 State of the Nation address (tribal-government revenue including business ops).
  • Seneca Nation — Tribal Business News May 2025 cited an "economic impact" figure of $1.9B (NOT pure revenue; includes indirect effects). Flagged MEDIUM confidence.
  • Winnebago / Ho-Chunk — Ho-Chunk, Inc. 2024 Annual Report.
  • Nakupuna — 2024 GovCon Awards finalist range ($75M-$300M); midpoint used.

Because disclosure standards vary, the combined-rank table is an approximation, not a precise income statement. Trust the federal rank more than the combined rank for like-for-like competitive analysis.
