Strategic positioning takeaway

Winnebago / Ho-Chunk runs a two-arm BD organization: All Native Group (the formal federal-services division of Ho-Chunk Inc., 11 audited staff including Sr Director Proposal Operations [name withheld] ex-Cherokee Federal) and Flatwater Group (11 audited staff led by VP of BD & Operations [name withheld] and Director of BD - Professional Services [name withheld], plus 3 BD managers, 2 capture managers, 3 proposal staff, and a National Sales Manager). Plus a 2-person parent-level Ho-Chunk Inc. sales arm. Total: 24 audited / 17 BD funnel / 7 Sales/Account. The portfolio mixes federal-services contracts with material industrial-manufacturing activity distinctive within the audited cohort. Top NAICS by award count: Landscaping Services (43), Nonferrous Metal Manufacturing (42), Building Finishing (32), Rolled Steel Manufacturing (29), Office Furniture (20) — tribal-owned manufacturing arms competing for federal-base improvement, GSA-MAS furniture procurement, and tribally-set-aside construction. Federal-services side is anchored by Dynamic-HHS JV ($192M ceiling, joint venture not wholly-owned) and Flatwater Professional Services ($88M). Bookings are $0.38B FY25-present ($0.32B services, $0.07B product = 19% of total). Federal #37 by ceiling ($0.68B FY22-25) — smallest federal book in the audited cohort. Archetype caveat: the Tribal-Industrial Diversified label captures a real structural pattern (federal services + owned industrial subsidiaries) but should be read alongside the small federal scale and the JV-dominated services book. Services still account for 84% of bookings; the industrial tail is meaningful (19%) but not a balanced dual engine.

Five things this report establishes about Winnebago / Ho-Chunk:

  1. Two-arm BD organization: All Native Group + Flatwater Group. The audited bench splits cleanly across two operating entities: All Native Group (federal-services arm of Ho-Chunk Inc., 11 staff — [name withheld] Dir Capture Mission Support, [name withheld] Sr Dir Proposal Ops, [name withheld] Proposal Mgr, [name withheld], [name withheld] [name withheld] BD AE, etc.) and Flatwater Group (11 staff — [name withheld] VP BD/Ops, [name withheld] Dir BD Pro Services, [name withheld] + [name withheld] BD Mgrs, [name withheld] + [name withheld] [name withheld] Capture Mgrs, [name withheld] + [name withheld] + [name withheld] Proposal staff, [name withheld] National Sales Mgr, plus 2 Flatwater Communications Sales). Plus 2 Ho-Chunk Inc parent-level Sales ([name withheld], [name withheld]). The Flatwater Group bench was missed entirely in the original Phase 1 scan and only surfaced via the 2026-05-20 URL-coverage audit.
  2. Industrial NAICS dominate the top of the portfolio. Top 5 NAICS by award count: Landscaping (43), Nonferrous Metal Mfg (42), Building Finishing (32), Rolled Steel (29), Office Furniture (20). This is a tribal-owned-industrial-manufacturing profile distinctive in the audited cohort — no other audited tribe shows this NAICS shape. Likely federal-base landscaping contracts, GSA furniture procurement, and tribally-set-aside steel/finishing work — likely supported by separate operational staff at Bear Mountain Services / HCI Management Services / Fire Creek Company rather than the federal-services BD bench.
  3. Dynamic-HHS JV ($192M) is the dominant revenue anchor — and it's a JV, not a wholly-owned subsidiary. Dynamic-HHS JV, LLC at $192M ceiling is the top recipient and represents ~60% of the $0.32B FY25-present services book. The 'JV' suffix indicates a joint venture (likely with a non-tribal partner for additional capability/credentialing) — distinct from the cohort's wholly-owned-subsidiary norm. Concentration risk: any deterioration in the JV-partner relationship would compress the federal-services book far more than the industrial-manufacturing lines could backfill. The two-arm BD headcount story (24 audited / 17 BD funnel) suggests structural depth, but the revenue is heavily anchored to one external-partner-dependent vehicle. Worth tracking the JV's customer mix and recompete schedule.
  4. BD bench size now mid-cohort, not the smallest. 24 mapped / 17 BD funnel staff. Tied with BSNC for smallest BD funnel among the $1B+ tribes but well above what the original scan suggested (8 BD funnel). Productivity per BD funnel staffer: $0.32B services / 17 BD-funnel staff puts Winnebago in the broad-coverage productivity-floor tier on the FY25 run-rate, alongside Cherokee, Seneca and the Scale Primes. The lower productivity number reflects the discovered bench expansion, not a structural change in the federal book.
  5. Cross-tribal-corp move from Cherokee Federal: [name withheld], Sr Dir Proposal Operations. [name withheld] joined as Senior Director Proposal Operations at All Native Group from Cherokee Federal (Tier 1 Proposal Operations Director, 3.2 yrs); earlier in her career she was at Booz Allen Hamilton for 10 years (1993-2003). The only audited senior bench member with both cross-ANC and prior-prime credentials — brings both Cherokee Federal proposal-operations playbook and (older) Booz-tier capture credentials.

Methodology

How this report was assembled

  1. Phase 1 — Discovery. LinkedIn employee scans of all ecosystem entities using job-title filters (Business Development, Capture, Strategy, Growth, Proposal, Market Intelligence, Sales, Account Executive, Account Manager).
  2. Phase 2 — Profile enrichment. Each prioritized profile pulled with full work history, education, prior employers (typically 6-9 past roles).
  3. Validation. Web search applied to sparse-profile cases to detect false positives (company-name collisions, departed-employee LinkedIn lag).
  4. Classification. Auto-classified prior-employer mindset against curated keyword lists. Manual review for edge cases.
  5. Cross-analysis. Department bucketing, recent-hire velocity, competitor-alumni clustering, vehicle/program alumni detection, military education signal, vertical coverage from current titles, and connection to known federal contract portfolio.

Coverage

  • 24 BD leaders fully enriched. Estimated total BD-funnel population: ~24-30.
  • Profile sparseness: ~5% of senior executives maintain minimal LinkedIn presence (cross-verified via secondary sources where possible).
