Strategic positioning takeaway

Chenega is the cohort's purest services-only operator. 97% of FY25-present awards are services ($1.15B of $1.16B); the product book is $0.01B (effectively zero) and there are 0 audited Sales/Account staff. The portfolio is a multi-vertical 'people-as-service' model spanning three distinct NAICS lines, not a single capability: Security Guards led with 114 FY25-present awards, Facilities Support (57), and Temporary Help (53). Chenega Global Protection ($243M) is the flagship security-services subsidiary. The three lines share security-cleared agency customers but are not interchangeable under a single technical stack — Chenega sits closer to a scaled staffing-and-facilities specialist with security as the anchor than to a pure single-vertical operator. The BD bench is structurally different from the prime-poach playbook ASRC and Cherokee run: Chenega has only 2 large-prime senior alumni vs ASRC's 21 — instead, Chenega builds from within (5 INTERNAL 10+yr lifers including [name withheld] SVP Growth 18 yrs, [name withheld] Director BD 24 yrs) plus selective cross-ANC additions ([name withheld] from Akima, [name withheld] from Seneca Holdings VP BD). Federal #5 by ceiling ($4.0B FY22-25). The structural ceiling worth tracking: guard-hours and FTE-month delivery models have lower velocity and margin headroom than IDIQ task-order vehicles, and the deliberate zero-product-motion choice limits valuation leverage relative to peers who layer product or managed-service elements on top.

Five things this report establishes about Chenega Corporation:

  1. Pure services-only operator — 97% services, zero meaningful product motion. Services book = $1.15B of $1.16B total ($0.01B product). 0 audited Sales/Account staff (vs Cherokee 4, Akima 13). Chenega has structurally chosen NOT to compete in the IT-reseller / product motion. The BD organization is purpose-built for services capture, proposal, and recompete defense.
  2. Three distinct NAICS lines under a 'people-as-service' umbrella — not one capability. Top 3 NAICS: Security Guards (114 awards), Facilities Support (57), Temporary Help Services (53). The three lines share security-cleared agency customers but are not interchangeable under a single technical or operational stack. Chenega Global Protection LLC ($243M ceiling) is the largest subsidiary by far and anchors the security line. Pricing model = guard-hours and FTE-months rather than IDIQ task orders — a different go-to-market than Akima/ASRC's technical-services play. Within the Vertical Depth Specialist archetype, Chenega sits closer to multi-vertical staffing-and-facilities specialist than pure single-capability operator.
  3. Anti-poach bench: built from within, not from primes. Only 2 large-prime senior alumni (Leidos, Deloitte) in the entire 42-person bench. Compare to ASRC (21) and Cherokee (36 LARGE-mindset total). 7 INTERNAL 10+yr lifers carry the senior bench (post-2026-05-28 EH&F entity rescan): SVP Growth [name withheld] (18 yrs), Director of BD [name withheld] (24 yrs), Sr Director of Operations [name withheld] Kazemi (19 yrs), Chief Development Officer Military/Intel [name withheld] (11 yrs), Sr Director BD/BI [name withheld] (11 yrs); plus the proposal-side leaders surfaced in the EH&F rescan: SVP Proposal Development [name withheld] (16 yrs) and Director Proposal Development [name withheld] (14 yrs). The newly-discovered EH&F entity (Chenega Corporation EH&F) is the proposal/BD hub, also adding SVP Business Development [name withheld] (7 yrs at Chenega with prior ANC experience).
  4. Cross-ANC selective additions: Akima + Seneca Holdings poaches. [name withheld] (Director of Mission Growth and Partnerships, ex-Akima Sr Capture Manager - Emerging Markets 1 yr). [name withheld] (Director, ex-Seneca Holdings VP Business Development 4 yrs). Two senior-level cross-ANC additions in a deliberately small-aperture bench. [name withheld] is notable — Seneca Holdings is now in Chenega's senior BD bench, which would inform the Seneca-vs-Chenega competitive dynamic on shared pursuits.
  5. Customer portfolio leans DoD + DHS + NASA (security-clearable agencies). DoD $433M, NASA $225M, DHS $165M, HHS $129M, Interior $43M. NASA at $225M is unusually high (#2 funder) and fits with NASA security/facilities-support contracts. DHS at $165M reflects Chenega's security-guard footprint at federal buildings. The portfolio composition (DoD + NASA + DHS) is consistent with the 'people-as-service' security/facilities staffing model in cleared facilities.
  6. Pricing-model ceiling worth tracking. Guard-hours and FTE-month delivery contracts are inherently lower-velocity and more labor-margin sensitive than IDIQ task-order vehicles that allow scope expansion and technology insertion. In a market where even traditional services primes are layering product or managed-service elements to improve multiples and stickiness, Chenega's near-total absence of product motion and zero Sales/Account staff creates a structural ceiling on both growth rate and valuation leverage that the 'Federal #5 by ceiling' positioning obscures. The high INTERNAL share (32% — cohort high) reinforces execution consistency but also limits the external market-sensing that prime-poach benches at ASRC and Cherokee provide. The 18-24 month test: does Chenega add a managed-services or technology-augmented layer to its security/facilities core, or stay locked into the labor-cost-pass-through model?

Methodology

How this report was assembled

  1. Phase 1 — Discovery. LinkedIn employee scans of all ecosystem entities using job-title filters (Business Development, Capture, Strategy, Growth, Proposal, Market Intelligence, Sales, Account Executive, Account Manager).
  2. Phase 2 — Profile enrichment. Each prioritized profile pulled with full work history, education, prior employers (typically 6-9 past roles).
  3. Validation. Web search applied to sparse-profile cases to detect false positives (company-name collisions, departed-employee LinkedIn lag).
  4. Classification. Auto-classified prior-employer mindset against curated keyword lists. Manual review for edge cases.
  5. Cross-analysis. Department bucketing, recent-hire velocity, competitor-alumni clustering, vehicle/program alumni detection, military education signal, vertical coverage from current titles, and connection to known federal contract portfolio.

Coverage

  • 43 BD leaders fully enriched. Estimated total BD-funnel population: ~34-42.
  • Profile sparseness: ~5% of senior executives maintain minimal LinkedIn presence (cross-verified via secondary sources where possible).

Refresh cadence
