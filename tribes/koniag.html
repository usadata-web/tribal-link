Strategic positioning takeaway

Koniag runs a heavy IT-services concentration across multiple federal customers — not a pure IT-services play. The book is DoD-anchored (48%) with HHS (16%) and USDA (12%) as material secondaries, delivered primarily through IT-named subsidiaries: Koniag IT Systems ($291M ceiling), Koniag Data Solutions ($140M), Koniag Emerging Technologies ($86M); plus the broader Koniag Professional Services ($155M) and Arlluk [name withheld] Solutions ($88M). Top NAICS are clustered in computer services (Other Computer Services 33 awards, Computer Systems Design 23, Custom Computer Programming 10) plus management consulting (25). The senior bench shows a notable ASRC Federal → Koniag corridor: CGO [name withheld] (ex-ASRC Federal CTO) and VP BD [name withheld] (ex-ASRC Federal VP BD Federal Health, 4.5 yrs) are both ex-ASRC seniors. Within the Niche Vertical Specialist archetype, Koniag's specialty is the IT-services delivery model deployed across multiple agencies — structurally different from Goldbelt's narrow DoD-tactical concentration. Bookings are $1.00B FY25-present ($0.97B services); BD funnel of 34 + 5 Sales/Account staff. Federal #7 by ceiling ($3.2B FY22-25).

Five things this report establishes about Koniag:

  1. Heavy IT-services concentration delivered via IT-named subsidiaries (not a pure play). 3 of top 5 subsidiaries are IT-named: Koniag IT Systems ($291M), Koniag Data Solutions ($140M), Koniag Emerging Technologies ($86M). Top NAICS = Other Computer Services (33 awards), Computer Systems Design (23), Custom Programming (10), plus management consulting (25). DoD is still 48% of the services book, so IT-services is the delivery mechanism not the singular product; the differentiator is the IT-concentration across multiple agency customers (DoD/HHS/USDA), not a single-vertical IT specialty.
  2. ASRC Federal → Koniag senior talent corridor. Two senior BD leaders came from ASRC Federal: Chief Growth Officer [name withheld] (ex-ASRC Federal CTO) and Vice President Business Development [name withheld] (ex-ASRC Federal VP BD Federal Health, 4.5 yrs). Koniag has both top-of-house growth strategy (CGO) and federal-health-vertical capture lead from ASRC's federal-services arm — directly informing ASRC-vs-Koniag positioning on shared IT-services pursuits.
  3. HHS at $160M is a strong secondary federal-health footprint (cohort-leading among non-DoD-dominant tribes). HHS = #2 funder, but it's 16% of the services book versus DoD's 48% — a strong secondary, not a signature vertical. Combined with [name withheld]'s ex-ASRC Federal Health VP capture credentials, Koniag is positioned for HHS IT-services (CMS, NIH, IHS, BARDA). The $160M HHS book is larger than Cherokee's HHS ($63M) and Chickasaw's HHS ($154M) — cohort-leading non-DoD-dominant HHS-IT footprint.
  4. USDA at $117M is distinctive vs the cohort. USDA is #3 funder. No other audited tribe shows USDA in top-5. Likely USDA IT modernization or farm-services administrative-systems work through Koniag IT Systems. Suggests a niche customer concentration that other tribes are not competing for.
  5. Lean INTERNAL bench, prime-poach-light. Only 1 audited 10+yr INTERNAL lifer (Kim Homolka, VP, 17 yrs). Only 2 large-prime senior alumni (Deloitte, IBM). Koniag is building primarily through the cross-ANC poach pattern (ASRC Federal corridor) plus recent organic hires. Bench is thinner than the federal ceiling rank would suggest — productivity-heavy operating model.
  6. ASRC corridor sustainability question. Two senior leaders ([name withheld] CGO, [name withheld] VP BD Federal Health) from one source company is a real but narrow corridor. The narrative treats ASRC → Koniag as a structural advantage; the open question is whether this is a repeatable pipeline that will continue to deliver new hires, or a one-time migration of two specific individuals at a specific moment. If the corridor doesn't extend deeper or repeat in subsequent cycles, Koniag's ability to scale federal-health and IT capture without deeper prime-alumni or INTERNAL bench depth becomes a material risk. Worth tracking: any additional ASRC Federal → Koniag hires in the next 12-18 months.

Methodology

How this report was assembled

  1. Phase 1 — Discovery. LinkedIn employee scans of all ecosystem entities using job-title filters (Business Development, Capture, Strategy, Growth, Proposal, Market Intelligence, Sales, Account Executive, Account Manager).
  2. Phase 2 — Profile enrichment. Each prioritized profile pulled with full work history, education, prior employers (typically 6-9 past roles).
  3. Validation. Web search applied to sparse-profile cases to detect false positives (company-name collisions, departed-employee LinkedIn lag).
  4. Classification. Auto-classified prior-employer mindset against curated keyword lists. Manual review for edge cases.
  5. Cross-analysis. Department bucketing, recent-hire velocity, competitor-alumni clustering, vehicle/program alumni detection, military education signal, vertical coverage from current titles, and connection to known federal contract portfolio.

Coverage

  • 40 BD leaders fully enriched. Estimated total BD-funnel population: ~39-48.
  • Profile sparseness: ~5% of senior executives maintain minimal LinkedIn presence (cross-verified via secondary sources where possible).

Refresh cadence

This methodology is reproducible. A 60-day refresh cycle would surface departures, new hires, promotions, and any new cross-ANC talent moves.
