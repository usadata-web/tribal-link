The cohort thesis

The 11 audited tribes are not a homogeneous cohort. They cluster into eight structural archetypes — each tribe characterized by a distinctive operating model. Customer access — not bench composition — is the real moat. LARGE-prime-alumni density correlates with book size (r=+0.48) but the causality runs both directions; importing prime DNA lifts total book size, not per-bid size or per-staffer efficiency (LARGE % is negatively correlated with productivity per staffer, r=−0.58, n=11 — directional).

A note on the people data: Every BD-bench number on this site is sourced from LinkedIn via HarvestAPI title-keyword scans (Business Development, Capture, Growth, Strategy, Proposal, MI, Sales, Account Executive/Manager) of each tribe's federal-services subsidiary pages, followed by apimaestro full-profile enrichment. Known limitations: (1) staff with generic titles (e.g. "Senior Vice President" without a function qualifier) may be missed if the headline also lacks BD keywords; we use a headline-augmented title classifier to mitigate, plus manual additions for known seniors; (2) some staff may have left the tribe without updating their LinkedIn profile, inflating bench counts at the margin; (3) some senior leaders (especially long-tenured executives) don't maintain active LinkedIn presence at all and won't appear; (4) Phase 1 LinkedIn scans target each tribe's known subsidiary URLs — staff tagged at unknown subs are missed (see the methodology section for current URL coverage). Calibration anchor: for one tribe where the true BD-funnel headcount is independently known, this methodology captured ~82% of it — the ~18% gap being staff whose LinkedIn titles don't surface a BD function, staff with no LinkedIn presence, or profiles hidden from the company directory. The published counts are best read as auditable lower bounds at roughly the 80–90% range, not a census — and because the same constraint applies to every tribe, cross-tribe comparisons remain like-for-like.

Operating-model archetypes

Terms used throughout: mapped = the full audited BD bench · BD funnel = the pursuit-pipeline subset (BD, Capture, Proposal, Growth, Compliance/Strategy, MI) · Sales/Account = the separate product-side motion. Mapped = BD funnel + Sales/Account. Per-staffer productivity is measured against the BD funnel.

Eight structural archetypes

Each of the 11 tribes is characterized by a distinctive operating model. Some archetypes have multiple members; others have one. Membership is not the point — the archetype names the attributes that make a tribe's pattern coherent. The primary classification dimensions are (a) BD funnel thickness and per-staffer productivity, (b) customer concentration and specialty depth, and (c) talent-source structure (LARGE-prime alumni, INTERNAL longevity, cross-ANC poaching, etc.). Cross-cutting attributes (recent-hire velocity, mindset mix, Akima-feeder status) overlay the archetypes rather than defining them.

Four analytical observations the data forces

1) LARGE-heavy benches are associated with larger books but lower output per person.
(Association, not established causation — causality plausibly runs both ways, and large books mechanically depress per-staffer figures regardless of bench composition.)
Two correlations tell the story: LARGE % moves with total Services $B (r=+0.475) but moves against per-staffer productivity (r=-0.577). The four highest-productivity tribes (Bering Straits 5%, Goldbelt 13%, Chickasaw 14%, Koniag 28%) average 15% LARGE; the four lowest (ASRC 36%, NANA / Akima 45%, Cherokee Nation 48%, Seneca Nation 32%) average 40%. Importing prime-alumni density lifts what you can win in aggregate but doesn't make the bench more efficient — and often makes it less. (Correlations computed from current config data, n=11; at this sample size treat them as directional, not precise — r=-0.577 is robust to single-tribe deletion, the recent-hire relationship is not.)

2) Lean, focused execution is the primary driver of productivity — specialty is one proven path but not the only one.
The efficient mid-tier tribes with multi-year agency or domain concentration (Goldbelt, Koniag, Chickasaw, Nakupuna, Chenega) all maintain clear specialties that have held across FY22–26: Chenega NASA + multi-agency security; Goldbelt DoD-tactical concentration + owned product subs; Nakupuna federal management-consulting pure-play (0% LARGE); Chickasaw DoT-aerospace + HHS; Koniag non-DoD HHS book + USDA. The single highest-productivity tribe, BSNC, has no comparable long-term specialty — its OPM book is entirely FY25–26 and a small share of its four-year services total; its result reflects a lean 17-person bench against a recent contract step-up: BSNC's services book grew ~44% FY24→FY25 (the only tribe growing while the cohort contracted), so its productivity lead is concentration-driven and likely to normalize, not a durable structural edge. Broad-coverage tribes (ASRC, Akima, Cherokee, Winnebago, Seneca) sit at the productivity floor despite benches up to 5× larger.

3) Scale and efficiency are separate operating modes, not points on a single growth path.
The two largest services books in the cohort — ASRC and Akima — sit in the productivity floor tier. The highest-productivity tribes run lean benches; the floor tier runs the thick ones. The structural breakpoint sits around 50 BD-funnel staff: every tribe above it (Akima, Cherokee, ASRC) averages 35%+ LARGE and lands in the floor tier; every tribe below it averages under 26% LARGE and lands in the efficient or specialty tiers. Recent-hire velocity doesn't bridge the gap (r=+0.178, propped by a single outlier — non-pivotal). Choose the operating mode deliberately — treating them as a single growth ladder leads to the wrong investment thesis.

4) Sole-source share is the most direct measure of the customer-access moat — and it inverts the scale story.
Share of the current book won on a sole-source basis (a proxy for incumbency / relationship depth): highest at Nakupuna 87%, Seneca Nation 71%, Koniag 70%; lowest at Winnebago / Ho-Chunk 34%, NANA / Akima 31%, ASRC 21%. The pure-play and niche operators carry the deepest sole-source moats, while the largest broad-coverage benches (ASRC, Akima) win most of their book in open competition. This is the empirical backbone of the "customer access is the moat" thesis: the tribes that look weakest on per-staffer productivity (e.g. the high-sole-source niche players) often hold the stickiest books, and the scale players that dominate total ceiling compete hardest for each dollar. Read alongside the productivity tiers, not instead of them.

Methodology

A research dashboard tracking 11 tribal parent corporations in federal contracting. For each, we map: corporate events (acquisitions, divestitures, key personnel changes), federal contract trajectory (USAspending data via the tribal-8a-trend Fabric pipeline), and the BD organization (Capture, Business Development, Proposal Operations, Sales, Account Management, Market Intelligence) using a repeatable LinkedIn-discovery + profile-enrichment methodology. Output is refreshed every 60-90 days.

Two-phase research process:

  1. Phase 1 — Discovery. LinkedIn employee scans of all federal-active subsidiaries using growth-function job-title filters (Business Development, Capture, Strategy, Growth, Proposal, Market Intelligence, Sales, Account Executive, Account Manager). HarvestAPI on Apify.
  2. Phase 2 — Profile enrichment. Each prioritized profile is pulled with full work history, education, and prior employers (typically 6-9 past roles). Auto-classified into mindset categories (LARGE / SMALL / ANC / GOVT / INTERNAL). apimaestro/linkedin-profile-detail on Apify.
  • BD bench data: per-tribe audited Phase 2 LinkedIn profile data, filtered via the same chain as the per-tribe pages (verify_current_employer match + exclude_companies + exclude_profile_usernames + intern regex). LinkedIn source has known limitations — staff with generic titles can be missed, departed staff may not have updated profiles, some senior leaders don't maintain a LinkedIn presence at all. Manual additions used to fill known gaps.
  • Bookings data: tribal-8a-trend/data/seg_award_detail.parquet (FY25 + FY26 YTD window, base+options value, consolidated_owner roll-up). Parquet extracted 2026-05-14; ~21 days behind the live Power BI cut, so numbers may be 5-9% below the dashboard's current view.
  • Cross-ANC talent flow: senior-tier (SVP/VP/Director/Chief/President) audited staff whose prior experience includes another tracked ANC entity. "Other ANC" row covers tribes outside the cohort (Tlingit Haida, Aleut, UIC, Calista, Doyon, etc.).
  • Productivity: FY25 services value ÷ bd_funnel_total_staff, presented as capture-adjusted bands. FY25 only (a complete fiscal year); FY26 is excluded from the rate because it is partially posted and its captured share varies per tribe. Window totals elsewhere still span FY25+FY26.
  • Recent-hire velocity: % of the audited bench whose current role started within the last 24 months (per is_recent_hire).
  • Competitor alumni: substring match against ~30 federal-services primes + Big Consulting (Leidos, CACI, BAE, SAIC, Jacobs, Northrop, Lockheed, Raytheon, Peraton, ManTech, GDIT, KBR, Deloitte, Booz Allen, Maximus, ManTech, Accenture, Guidehouse, EY, PwC, KPMG, ICF, etc.). Word-boundary required for ≤5-char all-alpha keywords (avoids "EY" inside HonEYwell).
  • Product fingerprints: top NAICS by award count from per-tribe federal_portfolio.top_naics_fy25_to_present block. Hand-curated narrative annotation per tribe.
  • Analytical frame: the three-archetype framing and the customer-access-moat insight were developed with an external federal-contracting BD strategist consultation (Grok-4.3, 2026-05-20). Consultation log archived at tribal-research/wiki/grok-consultation-2026-05-20-synthesis.md.
